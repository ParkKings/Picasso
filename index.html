1881 - 1973

Pablo Picasso

Spanish painter, sculptor, and revolutionary artist who co-founded Cubism

Early Life & Education

Pablo Diego José Francisco de Paula Juan Nepomuceno María de los Remedios Cipriano de la Santísima Trinidad Ruiz y Picasso was born on October 25, 1881, in Málaga, Spain. His father, Jose Ruiz, was a painter and art teacher who recognized his son's exceptional talent at an early age.

By age seven, Picasso began receiving formal artistic training from his father. His skills developed so rapidly that his father famously gave up his own painting, recognizing that his son had surpassed him. At just 13 years old, Picasso was admitted to the Royal Academy of San Fernando in Madrid, Spain's foremost art school.

Quick Facts

  • Born: October 25, 1881
  • Birthplace: Málaga, Spain
  • Died: April 8, 1973
  • Works created: ~100,000+

Artistic Periods

1901–1904

Blue Period

Following the suicide of his close friend Carlos Casagemas, Picasso entered a somber phase. His paintings featured predominantly blue and blue-green tones, depicting themes of poverty, loneliness, and despair.

1904–1906

Rose Period

As Picasso found love with Fernande Olivier, his palette warmed with cheerful oranges and pinks. This period featured circus performers, harlequins, and acrobats, reflecting his renewed joy.

1907–1919

Cubism

Picasso co-founded Cubism with Georges Braque, revolutionizing art by fragmenting objects into geometric shapes and showing multiple perspectives simultaneously.

Legacy & Impact

Picasso remains one of the most influential artists of the 20th century, having created an estimated 50,000 artworks including paintings, sculptures, ceramics, and prints. His innovations in Cubism changed how we perceive art forever.
